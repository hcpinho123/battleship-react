# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two players sitting at the same computer, taking turns. They need to be able to hand off the device without seeing each other's board. Casual gamers, likely friends or family. Context is relaxed, recreational — a game night or quick session.

## Product Purpose

A browser-based two-player Battleship game. Players place their fleets in secret, then take turns firing at each other's hidden grid until one fleet is sunk. Success looks like a smooth handoff between players with zero confusion about whose turn it is and what the rules are.

## Brand Personality

Dramatic, tactical, atmospheric. The feel of a naval war room — tension, precision, secrecy. Not a children's toy; not a hyper-realistic military sim. Somewhere between a classic board game and a cinematic mission briefing.

## Anti-references

- Bright, colorful casual game UIs (Candy Crush energy)
- Flat SaaS dashboard aesthetics
- Generic "game portal" chrome (neon gradients on black, pixel fonts by default)
- Overcrowded UIs with too many simultaneous elements

## Design Principles

1. **Secrecy is sacred** — the UI should reinforce that each player's board is private. Transitions and handoff screens exist to protect that.
2. **Tension through restraint** — less decoration, more negative space. The drama comes from the game, not from the chrome.
3. **Clarity over cleverness** — the rules must be understood on first read. Every word earns its place.
4. **Atmosphere without noise** — dark, nautical, cinematic mood through color + type, not through particle effects or over-animation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Support prefers-reduced-motion. Text contrast ≥4.5:1 against backgrounds.
